Product eligibility & scope

Which asset classes are reportable under each regime. OTC = over-the-counter; ETD = exchange-traded derivatives. Scope follows official product annexes and technical standards.

Filter:
Key scope differences: EMIR covers OTC and ETD — the broadest derivative scope. CFTC covers swaps only; exchange-traded futures/options excluded from Part 45. MiFID II covers all financial instruments (widest, including cash bonds/equities) but is a transaction reporting regime with no lifecycle concept. HKMA and MAS cover OTC derivatives only, with phased product scope (IR Phase 1, Credit Phase 2, Equity/Commodity Phase 3).
CFTC FX Treasury exemption: The US Treasury Secretary exempted FX swaps and FX forwards from the Dodd-Frank swap definition — these are NOT reportable under CFTC Part 45. FX options, NDFs, and cross-currency swaps remain reportable swaps. Under EMIR, physically settled FX forwards and FX swaps are fully reportable. This creates major cross-border asymmetry for EU–US FX trades.
